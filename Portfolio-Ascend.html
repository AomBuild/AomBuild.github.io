Overview

Bouldering is one of my hobbies, whether it be climbing plastic rocks in a gym or going outoors and finding some real rocks to play with is an activity i find enjoyable.

During my experience in climbing gyms, its hard to know what routes are available, are unavailable, due for a resets or which gyms are good based on their routes difficulty, style and quality of holds.

There are exisiting apps out in the market, however I believe there is an opportunity to provide more transparency to what a gym offers. Providing Climbers a backlog and history of all the climbs they have done; as well as Route Setters the ability to have that same backlog: one, to showcase their portfolio and two, for them to look back over the climbs, the projects created.

Architecture Understanding the Problem

The problem is lack of communication from Gym Owners to Router Setters to Climbers and in reverse. However the solution needed has to achieve three things:

  1. Projects/Routes easily available for climbers to see.
  2. Route Setters to easily upload route to a platform.
  3. Gym Owners to manage the account

Three different users, each with a different goal and focus. This was uncovered through my sessions at various gyms throughout Victoria, but conversations with Gym Owners, Route Setter and other Climbers; all three communciated some of these issues to me, therefore i knew where to start looking.

Firstly I looked at the Competition and broke down what they had to offer, what was their focus, who were they targetting and how did this shape their experience?

Secondly I used the previous discussions and built Archetype #1 | Route Setter / Owner and Archetype #2 | Climber, reason for the combination of the Owner and Route Setter into one artifact was due to the similarity in focus and goals.

Afterwards I created empathy maps based off discussions to refine my understanding of these groups.

  1. Emapthy Map #1 | Route Setter / Owner
  2. Emapthy Map #2 | Climber

These helped to provide an additional level of how these two types of user think, I can always refer to these artifacts when it comes to making design decisions.

RocketDeveloping a Solution

Now that we have a pool of research to refer back to when devising solutions, we can look at building some rough concepts to understand how all this would work.

I did some basic Card Sorting #1 and Card Sorting #2 exercises based on the research collected to provide a hierachy of what most to least important.

After that I went straight to creating User Flow #1 and User Flow #2 to have a visual map of that experience from point a to point b. This was crucial to get right, without it, devising an experience this complicated would push out the timeline for this project.

Wall_ArtCompany Brand Aesthetics

This project i created the brand that would encompass this experience. Through exploration, i came to the brand Ascend, following the name, led to the percpetion i wanted to give the brand, creating an aesthetic that would appeal to the community i was designing this for.

I followed a typical flow for graphic design:

  1. I created a brief that would outline what the brand is about.
  2. Created a moodboard to capture the vision for the brand.
  3. Highlighted key text from the brief in combination with reference to create the logo.
  4. Two typefaces were picked, one to reflect the brand image and the other for legibility which is standard practice.
  5. Trial and error with colour.
  6. Adapt the orginal screens to follow the brand guidelines.

Conclusion

This project was more difficult, considering this involved creating flows for regular day to day tasks plus onboarding, the latter which did change elements of the flow, however would increase adoption rates due to the quick and easy method of signing up.

This project in particular was more technical due to what flows were accounted for, and designing for a mobile experience does tend to add more difficult due to the 'limited' screen infrastructure available.

However this product was successful when pitching to the various users recruited for this experience. Solved the problems identified and branding for the product was well received.
