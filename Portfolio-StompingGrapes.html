Overview

Stomping Grapes is a website that focuses on selling wine to two distinct user groups, an expert and a novice, two different opposing types of users with different needs, wants and viewpoints.

The target setting out here is to provide an experience that matches those two polarising groups, which typically in UX practice you tend to avoid as creating a product or software that appeals to everyone appeals to no one.

However I think this would be interesting project to tackle as many similar websites tend to appeal to various user groups, but usually towards the novice whereas this project will be doing the opposite. Bridging the two groups into this platform.

Architecture Understanding the Problem

Here the aim was to create a platform that would cater to our 'expert' user demographic, but be able to communicate all the complexity and nuance with wine to our 'casual' group too. In order to achieve this.

  1. Understanding what the nuance and complexity in wine are.
  2. Break down heavy jargon into text that is comprehendable for that 'casual' users.
  3. To avoid alientating either group, the UI needs to be easy to scan, the UX needs to provide a narrative to help guide user towards their end goal.

On paper, sounds straigtforward, in application this is a complex balancing act, where we are competing with towards different groups of people, and to achive balance requires research, prototyping and feedback to achive this delicate balance.

So the first part was to do some General Research for e-commerce platforms, this would provide me a direction to go forwards with in regards to how websites in this area are structured; analysing other wine shopfronts was the next step to help refine the structure down.

At this point, it was necessary to conduct interviews with the 2 participants i recruited for this project. Two Archetypes where created, Wine Snob and Caual Enjoyer

Archetypes are just a start, we have to understand these users since they are both different, so whereas Archetypes are good to have an intiial idea of the user and their goals, needs, wants, fustrations and habits.

Empathy Map that helps us understand at a point in time what both parties see, think and feel. Rather than have two separate maps, i combines the overlaps in thoughts, feelings and actions; this allowed to simplify the early iteration work.

Whereas I split these based on user type as it was easier to overlap elements. Card Sorting (Wine Snob) | Card Sorting (Casual Enjoyer) to see what each group prioritises when making their decision on what wine to pick, these help to determine the UI which will then impact the UX of the platform.

RocketDeveloping a Solution

Once those artifacts are established, that is when you can go from understanding the problem to Developing a solution.

So here is where we take a solution focus mindest once we list all pain points and issues here, we devise artifacts such as:

  1. User Flow which will be used to visualise point A to B, devising a map for the Flow of the site.

  2. A & B Testing this testing method is typically misrepresented on social media and in bootcamps, typically because they boils down to two choices and whichever is most popular is the best UI pattern.

    But by addressing the differences, rationalising each choice (Pros & Cons); combined with other artifacts like card sorting, you learn what the user needs versus wants through comparing their behaviour and attitude.

Design_ServicesDesigning the Concept

This project is different compared to others. We esentially know the most efficient and over engineered (design-wise) flow, this is because of the abundence of online shopfronts, and similar to following a design system, these sites will adopt the same (if not very similar) flow, components or architecture, such as:

  1. Checkout Page
  2. Product Card
  3. Site Map

These are just some examples, however brands will adjust any of these due to company requirements or user research and therefore distinguishing themselves amongst competition (for better or worse).

Wall_ArtCompany Brand Aesthetics

Branding is not necessarily a focus when it comes to User Experience, but for User Interface it is important due to how the UI impacts the companys brand perception.

A company that utalises design language for their brand needs consistency, needs a clear direction for how the brand will look, feel and behave. Look and feel are impacted by the aesthetic of the UI whereas behaviour is the Interaction, how that UI responses to a user Input, and that is important for two reasons:

  1. If the UI does not behave to the users input correctly, this will impact negatively on the brand.
  2. The ill behaviour of the UI can affect the User Experience by disrupting the journey, therfore less sales.

Therefore it was essential to ensure those three elements (Look, Feel and Behaviour) all work in unision.

Conclusion

The aim to create an experience that could work for the two distinct user groups discovered was not necessarily an easy one. As previously mentioned, this is atypical when desigming products/softwares for user markets, creating a unicorn experience/product that does everything.

However this project was an experiment, ultimately a test to see whether this could be feasible to do in the future. The process was tumultuous because of the back and forth testing between two different parties and striking a balance that could work for both candiates.

But trial and error worked in the end, through usability testing, discussions and sessions, the solution was found, finding that balance between the two Archetypes, ensuring a platform that could be navigated, users can achieve their goals, and create a platform that becomed ingrained in a user journey when shopping for wine and alcohol.
